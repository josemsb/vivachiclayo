The Drinks to Try
Chicha Morada
This is a typical drink of Peru made of purple corn, pineapple, cinnamon, cloves, and sugar. You cannot leave without trying this, as it is one of the most common Peruvian beverages. Ask for it at almost any restaurant to experience a fun treat that dates back to Pre-Incan times!
Champús
Popular in Peru, Ecuador, and Colombia, this sweet drink is made of maize, cinnamon, cloves, and fruits such as pineapple, quince, apple and guanábana. Typically, champús is drunk warm and like Chicha Morada, is sold in many restaurants around Chiclayo.
Soya
A type of soy milk that is served warm. Soy is a popular ingredient grown in Peru.
Juices
Different types of freshly-squeezed juices are usually available at most restaurants. Pick from almost any freshly squeezed fruit such as orange, papaya, passion fruit, granadilla, and tuna.

The drink of Lambayeque, this drink is a twist off the traditional Peruvian Pisco and includes milk, algarrobo (a carob plant grown in the area), and pisco.
