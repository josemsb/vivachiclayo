The “King Tut of Peru”:
The “Museo Tumbas Reales”, or the Museum of the Royal Tombs of Sipán, consists of the Moche treasures discovered with the Lord of Sipán and his entourage in a tomb found in 1987. This unique, state-of-the-art museum is in the shape of a pyramid, reflecting the typical Moche “huaca”, and has 3 levels to tour. After the top level, which introduces the fascinating Moche culture, guests can descend to the bottom two floors where hundreds of breathtaking treasures from the actual tombs are displayed. Opened in November of 2002, this museum displays wonders found from one of the richest burial sites uncovered since the discovery of King Tutankhamen! This site not only holds the museum: it is home to a series of buildings where archaeologists are still restoring, recording, and preserving hundreds of artifacts from the find. The surrounding land also holds typical crops of the Moche people. Come visit and unearth the abundance of treasure, culture, and history found in the tomb for yourself!

Activities
If you have time, visit the buildings behind the museum. Take a look at the native crops growing and maybe even get a look at archaeologists at work cleaning and restoring ancient artifacts.

Interesting Facts:

-The Moche people believed in gods who were half animal, half man. At the museum, you can see artwork representing these deities– half man, half crab, fish, wolf, etc.

-The Lord of Sipán was buried with several animals, including a dog and llamas!

-Many treasures were taken by grave-robbers before the official discovery of the tombs. However, several artifacts were recovered in the Miami airport by the FBI and are now displayed in a special section of the museum.
