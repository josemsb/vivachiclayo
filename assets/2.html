A Town of Pirates, Wealth, and Afro-Peruvian Slaves
Zaña (or Saña) is home to the Afro-Peruano Museum that features the history of Afro-Peruvian slaves that lived in the area during the 1600s. At this time, the city of Zaña was a rich and booming city, as it controlled a key passage along the coast and was a crossroads of many heavily-trafficked routes. Unfortunately, word spread of their wealth, and in 1668 it was sacked by pirates, causing many wealthy families to leave the area. In 1720, what remained of the town was almost completely destroyed when it was hit by a flood. The current museum features every aspect of the Afro-Peruvian life, displaying everything from paintings and musical instruments to torture devices and masks. While visiting the museum, you can see the locals perform their traditional dances, and even learn a few! In addition to the museum, there are amazing ruins just a few minutes walk away, including a gorgeous convent built by the Spaniards. If you are feeling adventurous, take a walk across the suspended bridge near the ruins! Come visit the quaint town of Zaña, mingle with the population of Afro-Peruvians, and experience it’s unique history of ancient wealth, pirates, and slaves!

Interesting Facts:

-A popular dance in Peru, the Negra, comes from the dancing of Afro-Peruvians in the Zaña area.

-Zaña was famous for its wealth,  famous enough to reach the ears of the pirates who infested the Pacific waters. In March of 1686, the British pirate Edward Davis invaded the city, taking the people by surprise and taking Bella Mencia, a wealthy lady, captive. Story has it that they fell in love, and Bella happily fled with her captor.
