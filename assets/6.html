Museum, Pyramids, and Artisans
The site of the “Museo de Sitio Huacarajad de Sipán” contains a variety of exhibits to enjoy. This location boasts the famous Huaca Rajada, as well as other pyramids that are currently being prepped for excavation. It is home to the actual excavation sites of the famous Lord of Sipán, as well as the Old Lord of Sipán. You will have the opportunity to visit the dig sites, which contain replicas placed where the originals were found years ago. The actual museum exhibits ceramic and metal pieces of the Lambayeque, Cupisnique, and Moche people, as well as preserved skeletons and other ancient artifacts. Outside of the museum is an artisan market as well as a traditional restaurant. Come visit and enjoy all aspects of this ancient culture– everything from their burial grounds to their metalwork and ancient craft!
Location
Located in the village of Sipán, about 20 miles (31 km) from Chiclayo. The drive takes approximately 45 minutes.

Gift Shop
There are a few artisan shops right outside of the museum that sell replicas, jewelry, purses, and other artisan crafts. There is also a small restaurant that sells traditional food.
Activities
At the end of the museum, there is an area where you can take pictures with some props. They also have ceramic replicas that you can touch and hold.

Interesting Fact: Archaeologists are currently preparing to begin excavation on another pyramid close to the museum. Who knows what kind of artifacts and history will be unearthed here!
