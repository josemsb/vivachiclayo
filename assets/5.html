Birthplace of Lambayeque Culture
Huaca Chotuna is believed by locals to be the birthplace of Lambayeque culture. One of the most important fragments remaining of its culture is found on an ancient facade painted red, yellow, and white at the temple at Huaca Gloria. Visitors may view the pyramids (or “huacas”) from a distance at an overlook near the museum, and hear the exciting tale of the legendary Naylamp, the mystic founder of the Lambayeque culture. These murals, facades, pyramids, and museum give a rare glimpse into the Lambayeque culture in the place where it all began!

Interesting Facts:

-This is the site where the famous Tumi knife is held. Tumi is the official symbol of Lambayeque!

-Many bones and remains of sacrifices were found at the site--33 burials, most of them being women. One woman still had a fetus inside of her.
