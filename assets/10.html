Sicán Culture Unearthed
The National Sicán Museum is mainly dedicated to the Lambayeque (or Sicán) culture that developed in the region between 700 and 1300 AD before falling under Chimú rule. This museum displays artifacts that come from archaeological excavations, many of which occurred in the Bosque de Pomác. The Sicán culture eventually collapsed due to a long drought, which explains why many of the tombs represented in this museum are a result of sacrifices made to the gods in an attempt to bring rain. This museum has many stunning archaeological pieces that explain the culture of this society, and brings the Sicán people to life right before your eyes!

Interesting Facts:

-One of the tombs was booby-trapped with poisonous gas that would be released by opening it. Luckily, it had dissipated because of centuries of rain seepage before the archaeologists got to it.

-One of the tombs features a man buried upside down in the fetal position, symbolizing being in the womb and being born into the afterlife.
