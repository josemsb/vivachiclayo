Bird-watching Mecca:

The Bosque de Pómac, widely known as the home of an array of pyramids from the ancient Sicán civilization, is also an ecological wonderland just 40 minutes from the heart of Chiclayo. It is one of only four historic reserves in Peru, making it a must-see destination. It is the world’s largest equatorial dry forest, and is made up of mostly algorrobo (carob) trees. From these trees, local people can produce a myriad of different things: honey and carob syrup, furniture, charcoal, dye, firewood, sweetener, and medicine.

This forest is also the home to 70 bird species, 7 mammal species, and 9 reptile species. Being called an “unforgettable mix of nature and history”, it is an excellent place to gobird-watching, hike to the pyramids, ride horses, or just spend a day admiring the pyramids while relaxing under the trees!

Activities:

1. Hiking: There are over 34 different pyramids and ruins in the Bosque de Pómac, many of which can be reached by short hikes along the dirt paths and roads. Some of the pyramids you can even hike to the top of– it will be one “summit” that you will never forget.

 

2. The Millennial Tree: Come see this impressive Algorrobo tree that is said to be nearly a millennium old, and has many miracles and legends attributed to it by the local people.

 

3. Bird Watching: Watch for the over 70 species of birds in the area. Visit here for more information on this site and which species to look out for. Rancho Santana also offers bird-watching guided by one of their employees for more of an organized expedition.

4. Horseback Riding: Rancho Santana also offers tours by horseback through the forest to the top destinations.

5. Cultural Demonstrations: Outside of the protected area of the forest, there are many places where you can learn to weave, do ceramic work, or try some typical food of the area. Tell your tour guide what you specifically want to do, and they will likely know the best place to go.

 

Interesting Fact: There are still some Lambayeque source populations living nearby, many of which are present near the site selling artisan crafts and cooking traditional meals.

Tips: Be sure to bring sunblock and bug spray as well as close-toed shoes.
