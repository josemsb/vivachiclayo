An Animal Lover’s Paradise
The Chaparri Reserve is located in the dry forest of Lambayeque and is home to many rare animals, including the Andean/spectacled bear, white-winged guan, guanaco, and Andean condor as well as 196 other recorded species of birds! The Chaparri forest requires a guide to enter, and calling ahead is recommended since it is limited to just 30 visitors per day. This park features 3 visitor trails, as well as a vulture feeding station. Other animals include iguanas, pumas, and deer. Be sure to visit this reserve for the opportunity to see unique animals in an exclusive environment shared with just a few other tourists!
Activities
Spectacled Bear Rescue Center
This center houses 7 bears who have been rescued from the wild. Here you can have the rare opportunity to “adopt” a bear ($50 per year for a certificate, description, and photo). Regardless of whether or not you choose to do this, take the time to see and get up close to this unique species!
Herpetarium
The reserve has an on-site “snake house” which was created to educate visitors on the different types of snakes in the area. If you are feeling adventurous, take a stroll through this building and admire all of the colorful snakes.
Llama Treks (NOT YET OPEN)
In the future, the reserve will have llama-supported treks. This will entail about an 8-hour hike with llamas from the area that assist in carrying your bags.
Chaparri Ecolodge
 Come spend a few nights at the Ecolodge situated within the reserve, and enjoy the peaceful solitude of the forest. As most visitors leave for the night, it will just be you and a handful of people enjoying the serenity of this beautiful forest!
